One priority for student services functions is accessibility of student services and the institutions ability to provide access to important services, such as food pantries. In this project, I use a common method
for determining access to campus food pantries (see https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1353829224000790 for details) and build a dynamic application for student services staff to understand accessibility of 
campus food pantries for students. Importantly, the application allows users to assess accessibility based upon the hours and days of operation of the institutions food pantries and allows the user to determine the relevant distance
to use in the coverage calculations. The application provides the KPI (percentage of students within NYC that are within the accessibile distance of an open food pantry). The map can also function as a decision-support tool
because it maps the concentration of students residences against the locations of zones of accessibility of those pantries. 

You can view the application here: https://joshw4288.shinyapps.io/food_pantry_coverage/. In the space below, I provide some explanation for the R code used to produce the application.
